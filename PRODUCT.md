## Users

Vela serves Vietnamese enterprise learning teams and the people they support: internal learners, instructors, department managers, L&D admins, organization admins, auditors, executives, partner learners, guests, and Vela platform operators. They use the product in busy work contexts where they need to learn assigned material, publish training content, manage organization structure, track compliance, protect internal knowledge, and report training outcomes across departments and audience scopes.

## Product Purpose

Vela is a multi-tenant enterprise LMS for Vietnamese organizations. It brings e-learning, instructor-led training, publishing, documents, gamification, AI-assisted content creation, organization-based access control, and reporting into one operating surface. Success means users know what to do next, admins can manage training at scale, leaders can measure effective learning hours, and content stays governed by tenant, role, audience scope, and department branch.

## Brand Personality

Professional, encouraging, and precise. The product should feel trustworthy enough for enterprise operations, friendly enough for everyday learners, and progress-oriented without becoming playful in a childish way. Voice should be Vietnamese-first, clear, concise, and action-focused.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic SaaS hero layouts, decorative purple gradients, overly rounded cards, emoji-led gamification, marketing-heavy dashboards, and interfaces that hide dense operational work behind oversized empty decoration. Avoid one-note color themes, glassmorphism, vague AI product copy, and any pattern that makes standard admin tasks feel unfamiliar.

## Design Principles

1. Show the next useful action: learners and admins should quickly understand what needs attention, what is already complete, and what can be acted on now.
2. Make permission and scope visible: organization, role, audience, and department boundaries should be legible in the UI because they are central to trust.
3. Keep density readable: the product must support tables, reports, and training operations without losing hierarchy or scan speed.
4. Treat progress as a first-class object: course progress, rank progress, required hours, and reporting periods should be visible and comparable.
5. Protect serious workflows: publishing, account creation, password change, and access changes should feel deliberate, confirmed, and auditable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast, visible keyboard focus, semantic forms and tables, reduced-motion support, text labels alongside color-based state, and Vietnamese typography with comfortable line height for diacritics. Learning and admin workflows should remain usable on mobile, with structural responsive behavior rather than relying on tiny text or hidden controls.
